Symptoms and Medicine Suggestions for Cold Fever !

Cold fever details:

Common Cold Symptoms:

  • Nasal Symptoms: Runny or stuffy nose, sneezing.
  • Throat: Sore or scratchy throat.
  • Other: Mild cough, low-grade fever, body aches or mild headache, general feeling of being unwell.
  • Fever: Fever is usually mild and not as high as with the flu.
  • Duration: Symptoms usually start 1 to 3 days after exposure and can last for several days to a week or two.

Medicine for Cold fever:

  • Acetaminophen (Tylenol): Effective for reducing fever and relieving pain, but doesn't target inflammation.
  • Ibuprofen (Advil, Motrin): A nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) that can help relieve fever, aches, and pain, and also reduces inflammation.
  • Aspirin: Another NSAID that can relieve pain and fever, but it's best to avoid it, especially in children and teens, due to the risk of Reye's syndrome.
  • Naproxen (Aleve): Another NSAID that can help relieve fever, aches, and pain.

“Feed a cold, starve a fever” is an adage that's been around for centuries. The idea most likely originated during the Middle Ages when people believed there were two kinds of illnesses. The illnesses caused by low temperatures, such as a cold, needed to be fueled, so eating was recommended.
